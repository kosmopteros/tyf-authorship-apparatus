# Reading sympathetically

## Overview

The sympathetic read reports the experience of reading the work, the way a thoughtful reader would. It is read-only. It changes nothing and proposes nothing.

This is one of three read-only stances toward the same text. The sympathetic read reports experience. `diagnosing-text` identifies defects. `auditing-adversarially` attacks. They do not replace each other.

## The disciplined move

Report what happened to you as a reader: where you leaned in, where attention slipped, where you felt the turn, where you were lost, what you carried away. Stay experiential. Do not slide into a fix-list, because the moment you propose edits you have left this pass.

Separate experience from prescription. "I lost the thread here" is a sympathetic read observation. "Move this sentence up" is not; that belongs to `editing-faithfully`.

## Rationalization table

| What you will tell yourself | The reality | Do instead |
|---|---|---|
| "While I am here, I will suggest the fix too." | Mixing fixes into a read collapses three distinct passes into mush. | Report the experience; route fixes to the diagnosis pass or the Editor. |
| "A sympathetic read means saying it is good." | Sympathy is honesty about the experience, not flattery. | Report drift, confusion, and flatness as faithfully as delight. |
| "I should diagnose why it does not land." | The why is the diagnosis pass's job; this pass reports the what. | Say where it slipped; let `diagnosing-text` find the cause. |

## Red flags: stop if you catch yourself

- Producing a list of edits.
- Softening a genuine experience of drift to be kind.
- Explaining the structural cause instead of reporting the felt effect.

## Output shape

```markdown
## First reading
Where I leaned in:
Where attention slipped:
Where I felt the turn / the payoff:
Where I was lost:
What I carried away:
(No fixes. No edits.)
```

## Acceptance and edge cases

Correct behavior is more than a kind paragraph on clean prose. Hold these:

- **The piece is genuinely weak.** Report the honest experience (where it lost you, where it dragged) without sliding into a fix-list and without cruelty. False sympathy misleads the author.
- **The author actually wanted edits.** Deliver the read, then offer to switch to `diagnosing-text` or `editing-faithfully`. Do not pre-emptively blur into fixing.
- **The "piece" is one line or empty.** Say there is not enough text to read sympathetically yet; do not manufacture an experience.
- **The author is fishing for praise.** Sympathy is honesty about the experience, not validation. Give the genuine read.

## Next

If the author wants causes, go to `diagnosing-text`. If they want changes, go to `editing-faithfully`. If the work is near done, go to `auditing-adversarially`.
